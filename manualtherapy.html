Understanding Manual Therapy

Manual therapy involves hands-on techniques by trained practitioners to alleviate musculoskeletal pain and restore mobility. It is widely used in physiotherapy, osteopathy, and chiropractic practices and focuses on the body’s natural healing process.

Common Techniques:

  • Joint Mobilization
  • Soft Tissue Manipulation
  • Myofascial Release
  • Trigger Point Therapy
  • Muscle Energy Techniques

Conditions Treated:

  • Back pain and stiffness
  • Neck pain and headaches
  • Shoulder and knee joint injuries
  • Sports injuries and muscle strains
  • Frozen shoulder and postural imbalances

Example Treatments:

  • Problem: Ankle Sprain
    Treatment: Gentle oscillatory movements and joint traction to improve range and alignment.
  • Problem: Lower Back Pain
    Treatment: Lumbar mobilizations, stretching of tight muscles, and trigger point release.
  • Problem: Frozen Shoulder
    Treatment: Passive glenohumeral joint mobilizations and soft tissue massage.
  • Problem: Cervical Headache
    Treatment: Cervical spine mobilizations, suboccipital release, and posture correction.

Benefits of Manual Therapy:

  • Reduces muscle tension and pain
  • Improves blood circulation and healing
  • Restores joint movement and flexibility
  • Helps in faster recovery from injuries
